Jambalaya recipe

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup oil
  • 1 rotisserie chicken (chopped or shredded)
  • 1 pound pork sausage (cut into 1/4 inch pieces)
  • 1 teaspoon Tony's Creole seasoning(or any cajun seasoning you can find)
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 large onions, above average is fine too I guess
  • 2 large ribs of celery
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 can tomato sauce
  • another teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons chopped parsley
  • 2 tablespoons chopped green onion
  • 4 cups of cooked rice
  • 1/4 cup chopped bell pepper

Procedure

  1. Heat oil in a large thick pot on medium high heat.
  2. Add chicken pieces, brown on all sides and cook until tender.
  3. Add sausage, Tony's Seasoning and salt.
  4. Cover, lower heat and cook until chicken is well done, about 25-30 minutes.
  5. Remove everything and drain all 1/3 of the drippings from the pot.
  6. Add onions, celery, bell pepper and garlic and saut√© until tender.
  7. Stir in tomato sauce and salt and return chicken and sausage to the pot.
  8. Cover and simmer about 10 minutes.
  9. Add parsley and green onion tops, cooking an additional 5 minutes, covered
  10. Fold in cooked rice and let simmer for 10 minutes.
  11. Optionally
    • Add celery salt or stock for extra flavor.
    • Add a drained can of water chestnuts when you add the rice in for extra texture.

About Jambalaya

There are different stories about the origin of Jamabalaya but my favorite is about how a wealthy guest arrived too late for the usual dinner serving hours at a New Orleans inn. At a loss as to what to serve the owner turned to his head chef and said "Jean, Balayez" meaning, "blend some good stuff together."
Delighted with the results the guest suggest he serve it to other guests, which he did under the name "Jean, Balayez." This eventually merged to "Jambalaya" and is a savoury specialty of Bayou country, known for being made with whatever you have left in the kitchen.
